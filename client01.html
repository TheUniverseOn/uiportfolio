Redesigning a product

  • Marketplace

The client approached me to redesign their product research: He already has gathered all the research himself, so I was only responsible for researching the competitor’s product and get inspiration from them.


Skills

Project Canvasing, Stakeholder Management, User Interviews, Empathy Mapping, User Flows, Participatory Design, Experience Mapping, Sketching, Rapid Prototyping, Usability Testing, Visual Design.



Initial Conversation I had with the Client

"Who is your audience, and what are their goals?"

Our platform is for people interested in high industry products such as factory machinery, components, textiles, mechanical tools, robotics etc. These products are B2B products which go towards businesses procuring these products. Our target audience, therefore, are businessmen or procurement teams of small-medium size businesses looking to purchase these products to run their daily operations.

"What are the major pain-points of the current version?"

1. lacking a professional look and design
2. interface leaves a lot to be desired.
3. there is no reinforcement in the interface about our company's mission statement or the value we're trying to add

"What does success look like for the project?"

1. a very well designed dashboard interface with that makes making requests and sending offers easy and hassle free
2. makes interaction between buyers and sellers flowing and simple
3. an interface that reinforces our company's goal
4. Interface with attention to small details of the functional aspects of the interface e.g. rating system.

"Who are your competitors?"

Direct- Alibaba, indiamart, perfectchannel, maistro, eworldtrade
Indirect- Business directories, b2b catalogs

Design

Using design principles I presented my client with a wireframe to move forward. The client wanted a hands-on collaboration during the project, acted as product manager during the process. I had to collaborate with him throughout every step of the process from concept development to the final iteration. I was assigned with inspirations the client has already gathered before our engagement, using his research and my guidance, we discussed what should be taken from the lists of inspiration and what should be left out. Keeping the end user’s process very simple and minimal learning curve was the final decision, as the was the users were not tech-savvy. This brought us into the final the designs that are shown below. The client was very happy with the outcome, and they are working on the product with his developers.
